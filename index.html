Introduction & Problem Statement

Echocardiography remains the cornerstone of cardiac assessment, with apical 2-chamber (2CH) and 4-chamber (4CH) views being critical for evaluating left ventricular function, wall motion, and ejection fraction.

However, automated view classification is hindered by:

  • Acoustic noise & speckle patterns in ultrasound images
  • High inter-patient anatomical variability
  • Limited annotated datasets for deep learning
  • Real-time inference requirements in clinical settings

Traditional CNNs struggle with ultrasound-specific artifacts, while lightweight models lack robustness. EchoHybrid addresses this by combining a noise-resilient Compact Residual Dense Network (RDN) with state-of-the-art CNN backbones in a hybrid ensemble.

Preprocessing Pipeline

  1. Intensity Normalization: [0, 255] → [0, 1]
  2. CLAHE Enhancement: Contrast improvement in dark regions
  3. Center Cropping: Focus on cardiac chamber
  4. Resizing: 224×224 with bicubic interpolation

Ultrasound-Specific Augmentations

  • Speckle Noise: σ ∈ [0.01, 0.05]
  • Gaussian Noise: σ ∈ [0, 0.1]
  • Salt & Pepper: p ∈ [0.01, 0.03]
  • Elastic Deformation
